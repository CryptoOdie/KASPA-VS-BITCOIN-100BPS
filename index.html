Kaspa vs. Bitcoin

As block rewards decline through halvings, transaction fees must rise to maintain a secure and sustainable network. While there’s no fixed rule, history shows that dedicating around 3% of Bitcoin’s market cap has been enough to secure 51% of the network’s hash power. This 3% threshold isn’t unique to Bitcoin. It likely applies to any Proof-of-Work system designed to resist 51% attacks — including Kaspa.

If Bitcoin’s market cap is $1 trillion, then roughly $30 billion per year (or about $82 million per day) has historically been sufficient to deter a 51% attack.

If Bitcoin’s market cap reaches $100 trillion, then a proportionate security budget would be around $3 trillion per year (or ~$8.2 billion per day).

This isn’t wasted spending — it funds infrastructure. Unlike defending the U.S. dollar through military force, PoW security budgets largely support renewable energy mining. The result? More clean energy production, economic empowerment, and a monetary system that uplifts global prosperity.
