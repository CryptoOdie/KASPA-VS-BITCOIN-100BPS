üü¶ Network Congestion:
This slider controls how congested the blockchain network is. Congestion represents how many blocks are being filled with transactions. A network running at 100% congestion is processing the maximum possible number of transactions per day. In Kaspa‚Äôs case, this is based on block throughput (e.g., 30000 txs per second at 100 BPS). Higher congestion means higher total daily transactions, which increases fee revenue.

üìÜ Date:
This slider allows you to scrub through hypothetical dates or preset moments in time for educational and scenario-building purposes. It may also be used to anchor assumptions like fee environments or market states in future revisions of this calculator.

üíµ Average Fee Rate: USD (per Single Transaction):
This value determines the average fee paid for a single transaction. For example, if this is set to $10 and 1 million transactions occur, the total fee revenue is $10 million. This field is used to compute the Fee Revenue value. Kaspa‚Äôs scalability allows for lower fee rates with higher throughput.

üè¶ Market Cap:
Represents the total market capitalization of the asset being secured (e.g., Bitcoin or Kaspa). This is a critical input, as the calculator uses it to derive the suggested minimum security budget ‚Äî defined here as 2% of the market cap per year, divided by 365 to find the daily minimum.

üö® Suggested Daily Miner Revenue Minimum:
This red panel calculates a 2% annual security budget as a baseline for defending a proof-of-work blockchain. It divides 2% of the market cap by 365 to estimate the daily revenue miners must earn to keep the network secure against a 51% attack. If daily miner revenue is below this threshold, the network may be economically vulnerable to attacks.

üî∂ Block Reward:
The amount miners receive from new coins being minted (i.e., inflationary issuance). In Bitcoin, this amount halves approximately every 4 years. In Kaspa, it reduces gradually every month. Block reward is a fixed calculation independent of fee activity and is added to miner revenue.

üü© Fee Revenue:
The total amount miners earn from transaction fees. It is calculated as:
Fee Revenue = Number of Transactions √ó Fee Rate
This is directly influenced by network congestion and average fee rate. In a healthy system, this value should rise over time to replace falling block rewards.

üí∞ Daily Miner Revenue:
This is the total compensation miners receive per day. It is the sum of:
Daily Miner Revenue = Block Reward + Fee Revenue
This figure must ideally meet or exceed the Suggested Daily Miner Revenue Minimum to ensure network security is economically sustainable.

üîò Market Cap Slider: Adjusts total network value. Directly affects the 2% annual security budget calculation.

üîò Fee Rate Slider: Modifies the average fee per transaction. Combined with congestion, it determines fee revenue.

Kaspa vs. Bitcoin: Security Budget Simulator Overview

Date: This slider represents the progression of time. It adjusts Bitcoin‚Äôs block reward based on its halving schedule and adjusts Kaspa‚Äôs block reward to reflect its gradual monthly emissions reduction. Both blockchains decrease issuance over time, which means a greater reliance on transaction fees in the future.

Network Congestion: This slider lets you simulate different levels of network usage. It adjusts how many of the maximum theoretical transactions per day are actually occurring. For Bitcoin, the cap is based on ~7 TPS. For Kaspa, it is based on 10,000 TPS at 100 BPS (Blocks Per Second). This directly impacts total fee revenue.

Fee Rate (USD): This slider represents the average transaction fee per transaction, in USD. While transaction fees are paid in each blockchain‚Äôs native token (BTC or KAS), this simulation uses USD for cross-network comparison. This number multiplied by total transactions = total daily fee revenue.

Market Cap: This slider simulates the total market capitalization of the blockchain in USD. It is used to estimate a sustainable security budget based on a target percentage (e.g. 2%). For instance, 2% of a $1 trillion market cap = $20 billion/year or ~$54.8 million/day in security spend.

Security Budget: This reflects the minimum amount of miner revenue (from both block rewards and fees) required to protect the network against attacks like 51%. Historically, a few percent of market cap annually has been enough to secure Bitcoin.

Kaspa Specifics: Kaspa's emissions reduce monthly and it can process vastly more transactions per second than Bitcoin. This allows it to potentially generate a larger portion of its security budget from fees‚Äîespecially at high congestion and low latency settings. Annual inflation also declines over time.
